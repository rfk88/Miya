App Review — Third-party AI (Miya AI) and user consent (Guidelines 5.1.1(i) / 5.1.2(i))

New accounts
- Optional AI features that send limited wellbeing/health context to Miya AI are OFF by default.
- Users explicitly opt in during onboarding (Privacy & Alerts step) or later in Edit profile.
- Until they opt in, the app does not call our Miya AI–backed edge functions for chat, pattern insights, or message regeneration.

Existing users at release
- Accounts that already had a privacy_settings row are migrated server-side to remain opted IN so behavior stays consistent (with an in-app one-time transparency notice and an Edit profile toggle to turn AI off).
- The Privacy Policy describes Miya AI’s role; the in-app “Learn more” sheet names Miya AI and what categories of data may be sent depending on the feature.

Settings
- Edit profile includes a toggle: “Share limited data with Miya AI,” plus an info sheet and Privacy Policy link.

Server
- Edge functions that invoke the AI model (e.g. arlo-chat, arlo-member-chat, miya_insight, miya_insight_chat, regenerate_message) verify privacy_settings before calling the provider.
